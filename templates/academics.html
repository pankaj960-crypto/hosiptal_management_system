Department

At Madhubani Medical College, we offer a comprehensive range of academic departments designed to provide students with a robust foundation in both theoretical and practical aspects of medical science. Our curriculum is meticulously crafted in alignment with the latest NMC guidelines, ensuring a balance between modern advancements and essential core knowledge. We host pre-clinical, para-clinical, and clinical departments, each equipped with state-of-the-art laboratories, demonstration rooms, and research facilities. The Pre-Clinical Departments such as Anatomy, Physiology, and Biochemistry introduce students to the structural and functional basics of the human body. These are supported by advanced labs and cadaver-based dissection halls. The Para-Clinical Departments, including Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology, and Forensic Medicine, help students bridge the gap between theory and clinical application. They play a crucial role in diagnostic training and rational drug usage, preparing students for future clinical decision-making.

Preclinical Departments

ANATOMY
This department works closely with the other preclinical departments through horizontal integration to offer doctors a strong foundation.
BIOCHEMISTRY
The Department of Biochemistry seeks to advance the understanding of chemical structures and processes that constitute health and disease.
PHYSIOLOGY
The pre-clinical department of Physiology studies how the human body functions. Along with Anatomy and Biochemistry, it forms the foundation of basic medical sciences.
PHARMACOLOGY
Pharmacology is the study of how drugs interact with biological systems and their therapeutic uses in medicine.
PATHOLOGY
The Pathology department plays a crucial role in the diagnosis of diseases by examining tissues, cells, and bodily fluids.
MICROBIOLOGY
Microbiology is the broad term that deals with microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and their effects on human health.
FORENSIC MEDICINE
Through this study, students are provided with an overview of the medico legal aspects of health sciences, components of toxicology and ethics.
COMMUNITY MEDICINE
The Department of Community Medicine is well known for its value-based education and contribution to all strata of the society. Through teaching and training, and service and research initiatives, the Department contributes significantly to developing empowered physicians of first contact.
MICROBIOLOGY

Dermatology
Surgery is a medical speciality that uses operative manual and instrumental techniques to investigate and/or treat a pathological condition such as a disease or an injury, to help improve bodily function or appearance. Pre-operative and post-operative care come together at the Department of General Surgery.
EMERGENCY MEDICINE
Emergency Medicine is a specialty that provides care for emergency patients by specially trained doctors who deliver a wide range of services. It includes initial assessment and early treatment in resuscitation, trauma, and medical and pediatric emergency including surgery and anesthesia
GENERAL MEDICINE
Internal Medicine or General Medicine is the special branch and speciality of medicine that deals with the diagnosis and non-surgical treatment of diseases, and focuses on how to prevent, diagnose, and treat diseases that affect adults.
GENERAL SURGERY
Surgery is a medical speciality that uses operative manual and instrumental techniques to investigate and/or treat a pathological condition such as a disease or an injury, to help improve bodily function or appearance. Pre-operative and post-operative care come together at the Department of General Surgery.
Obstetrics & Gynecology
Obstetrics and Gynecology (OBG) are the two surgical specialties dealing with the female reproductive organs. Obstetrics is the surgical specialty dealing with the care of a woman and her offspring during pregnancy, childbirth and the puerperium (the short period after birth).
OPHTHALMOLOGY
Ophthalmology is the branch of medicine that deals with the diseases and surgery of the visual pathways, including the eye, brain, and areas surrounding the eye, such as the lacrimal system and eyelids.
